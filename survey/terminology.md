# Terminology

- **World model:** a learned representation of a dynamic system and its temporal transitions that enables prediction, generation, or simulation of possible future states.
- **State:** the observation-space, latent, physical, graph, or multimodal representation used to characterize the system at a time.
- **Transition model:** the mechanism mapping a state and optional action/context to a distribution or estimate over future states.
- **Rollout:** repeated or direct multi-step generation of a possible future trajectory.
- **Anomaly detection:** abnormality identification in the current or observed interval.
- **Early warning:** future-event risk estimated for a declared gap and horizon.
- **Prognosis:** prediction of degradation or failure evolution.
- **RUL:** time remaining until a defined failure condition.
- **Action-conditioned dynamics:** transitions conditioned on control, maintenance, or intervention actions.
- **Counterfactual:** a predicted trajectory under an alternative action or condition.

Terms should be operationalized by mechanisms and protocols, not inferred from titles alone.
